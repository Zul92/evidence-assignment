Section 53 Section 54 Section 55
Under Section 53 of the Evidence Act 1950, the good character of the accused is relevant in criminal proceedings.
"I have always been a law-abiding citizen. My reputation matters here!"
The Accused
Defense Counsel
The defense can adduce such evidence at any stage after the prosecution closes its case.
"Your Honor, since the prosecution has rested, we wish to lead evidence regarding the accused’s good character."
According to Section 55, only general reputation and disposition are admissible.
"He is known in the community for his honesty and integrity over the last 10 years!"
WARNING: Specific good acts are NOT admissible!
Witness (Employer)
Prosecution
Be careful! Opening the "shield" allows the prosecution to introduce bad character in reply under Section 54.
"Since the defense raised character, I shall now introduce the accused's bad character and prior records!"

Leading Malaysian Cases

Munuswamy Sundar Raj v PP

"Good character bolsters **credibility**, but does not outweigh direct proof of guilt."

Syed Ismail v PP

"Requires **positive proof**! Mere negative affirmations are insufficient for admissibility."

"Based on **Khoo Ban Hock v PP**, we consider your character as mitigation to reduce your sentence."
Finally, good character plays a vital role in **Mitigation and Sentencing**.
